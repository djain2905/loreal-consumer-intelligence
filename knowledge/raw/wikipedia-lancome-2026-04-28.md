Lancôme

**Lancôme** (French pronunciation:[\[lɑ̃kom\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French")) is a French perfume and cosmetics house that distributes products internationally.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-1) A subsidiary of [L'Oréal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%27Or%C3%A9al "L'Oréal"), Lancôme offers [skin care](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skin_care "Skin care"), [fragrances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fragrances "Fragrances"), and [makeup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makeup "Makeup").

## History

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lanc%C3%B4me&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: History")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Lancome_NYC_Boutique_Exterior.jpg/250px-Lancome_NYC_Boutique_Exterior.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lancome_NYC_Boutique_Exterior.jpg) Lancôme Boutique on NYC's Upper West Side

Founded in 1935 by [Guillaume d'Ornano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillaume_d%27Ornano "Guillaume d'Ornano") and his business partner Armand Petitjean in France, as a fragrance house. The name "Lancôme" was inspired by the forest of Lancosme in the region of [Brenne](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Brenne_(region)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Brenne (region) (page does not exist)") \[ [fr](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brenne_(r%C3%A9gion_naturelle) "fr:Brenne (région naturelle)")\], which lies in the Indre valley in the heart of France - the name was chosen by Guillaume's wife Elisabeth d'Ornano. The roses in the area inspired the company's symbol of the single golden rose.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-2)

Lancôme launched its first five fragrances in 1935 at the [World's Fair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brussels_International_Exposition_(1935) "Brussels International Exposition (1935)") in Brussels: _Tendre Nuit_, _Bocages_, _Conquete_, _Kypre_ and _Tropiques_. Petitjean entered into the skincare market, launching Nutrix, his first "all-purpose repair cream" in 1936, followed by make-up, cosmetics, and skincare products. Lancôme was acquired by [L'Oréal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%27Or%C3%A9al "L'Oréal") in 1964, and became part of its luxury products division.

## Advertising

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lanc%C3%B4me&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Advertising")\]

Despite founder Armand Petijean's assertion that Lancôme never advertise, today Lancôme is one of the top advertisers in the beauty arena. Its ads can be seen in numerous publications worldwide. Lancôme's ads have been shot by leading photographers, including [Peter Lindbergh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Lindbergh "Peter Lindbergh"), [Mario Testino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mario_Testino "Mario Testino"), [Mario Sorrenti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mario_Sorrenti "Mario Sorrenti"), [Nick Knight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Knight_(photographer) "Nick Knight (photographer)"), [Steven Meisel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Meisel "Steven Meisel"), [Brigitte Lacombe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brigitte_Lacombe "Brigitte Lacombe"), [Patrick Demarchelier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Demarchelier "Patrick Demarchelier"), and Dusan Reljin.

Fashion designers that have collaborated with the brand, include design duo [Proenza Schouler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proenza_Schouler "Proenza Schouler"), and [Alber Elbaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alber_Elbaz "Alber Elbaz")

One of Lancôme's longest-running partnerships was with actress [Isabella Rossellini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isabella_Rossellini "Isabella Rossellini"). Rossellini was the international face of Lancôme for 14 years. In 2016, Rossellini returned for a collaboration, more than 30 years after she became its first face.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-3)

In 2006, [Clive Owen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clive_Owen "Clive Owen") became the first male spokesperson for Lancôme. He joined to represent the brand's new Men's skincare range and the fragrance Hypnôse Homme.

## Controversy

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lanc%C3%B4me&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Controversy")\]

### Hong Kong

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lanc%C3%B4me&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Hong Kong")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Pan_Democratic_Boycott_Loreal_in_CWB_Times_Square_20160608.JPG/250px-Pan_Democratic_Boycott_Loreal_in_CWB_Times_Square_20160608.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pan_Democratic_Boycott_Loreal_in_CWB_Times_Square_20160608.JPG) Protesters gathered Times Square mall on 8/6 afternoon[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Lancome_Kiosk_in_CWB_Lane_Crawford_after_protest_20160608.JPG/250px-Lancome_Kiosk_in_CWB_Lane_Crawford_after_protest_20160608.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Lancome_Kiosk_in_CWB_Lane_Crawford_after_protest_20160608.JPG) Lancome counters in [Lane Crawford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lane_Crawford "Lane Crawford") after the protest

Main article: [Denise Ho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denise_Ho "Denise Ho")

On 5 June 2016, Lancôme canceled a promotional concert by Hong Kong [pro-democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pro-democracy_camp_in_Hong_Kong "Pro-democracy camp in Hong Kong") singer [Denise Ho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denise_Ho "Denise Ho") that was scheduled to be held on 19 June in [Sheung Wan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheung_Wan "Sheung Wan").[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-scraps-4) The cancellation was due to a [boycott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boycott "Boycott") campaign launched by the [Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China "Communist Party of China")-controlled _[Global Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Times "Global Times")_, which denigrated the [Cantopop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantopop "Cantopop") star for supposedly advocating Hong Kong and Tibet independence.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-scraps-4) Lancôme posted on [Facebook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facebook "Facebook") that Ho is not a spokesperson for the brand.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-5) The cancellation drew a heavy backlash in Hong Kong, resulting in some Lancôme shops in Hong Kong shutting down during the protests.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-6) Some Hong Kong merchants refused to accept the political pressure from the [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") government.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-7) [Listerine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Listerine "Listerine"), another brand that Ho represents, retained the singer despite criticism from the _Global Times_ for hiring Ho as its spokesperson.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-scraps-4)

Ho ended up playing the concert without Lancôme's support.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanc%C3%B4me#cite_note-8)
